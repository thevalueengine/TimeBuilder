Radical Candor 1-to-1s

Your Guide to a Great 1-to-1

How to prepare, what to think about, and prompts to help you build a great agenda. A great 1-to-1 is your protected space — come prepared with clear talking points.

💡

The golden rule

A great 1-to-1 is your time — about your work, your growth, your challenges, and what you need to thrive. Your manager's job is to listen and support. Your job is to come prepared.

1
Wins & Progress
Start with what's gone well — it sets the tone and shows your manager where you're gaining momentum.
What are you proud of from the last week / fortnight / month?
Where have you made progress your manager might not have seen?
Any moments where you felt "in flow" or energised — why?
What recent feedback have you put into practice?
2
Challenges & Frictions
This is where Radical Candor lives — honest, specific, and constructive.
What's felt hard or unclear recently?
Any work that is stuck, slow, or frustrating?
Any feedback you'd like to ask for?
Where do you feel you need more support, clarity, or resources?
Anything in your workload or environment that's getting in the way?
3
Growth & Development
About improving your craft and building your long-term path.
What skills would you like to strengthen right now?
Is there something you attempted recently you'd like coaching on?
Any habits you want to build or refine?
Are there responsibilities you'd like to take on in future?
4
Collaboration & Team Dynamics
Healthy teams grow together — this helps surface things early.
Anything about team communication you'd like to discuss?
Any cross-team friction or blockers worth raising?
Where have you seen great teamwork recently?
Anything you'd like from your manager to help collaboration flow better?
5
Feedback for Your Manager
Two-way Radical Candor is essential — they can't grow without your input.
What's working well about how they support you?
What would help you more?
Anything they could do differently to make your work easier or more impactful?
✓
Your Session Agenda
Two or three strong talking points with specific examples beats a long list every time.

Simple Agenda Format

1
Wins + Updates
2
Key challenges I'd like to explore
3
Feedback I'm seeking
4
Development / skills focus
5
Feedback for my manager
6
Any decisions or next steps needed
Manager Guide

How to Run a Great 1-to-1

Your preparation, your mindset, and the questions that turn a routine catch-up into a conversation that actually moves things forward.

🎯

The manager's mindset

A great 1-to-1 is not a status update. Your job is to listen more than you talk, ask better questions, and create space for your team member to think clearly. The agenda belongs to them.

1
Before the Meeting
Preparation separates a great manager from a reactive one. Give yourself five minutes.
What have you observed about this person's work since your last 1-to-1?
Is there specific feedback — positive or developmental — you want to bring?
Are there team or business updates they need context on?
What do you know about their current mood, motivation, or workload?
Are there commitments from last time you need to follow up on?
2
Opening the Conversation
How you start sets the tone. Avoid diving straight into tasks.
"How's your week been — what's on your mind?"
"What's the most important thing we should talk about today?"
"Before we go through your agenda — is there anything pressing I should know?"
"On a scale of 1–10, how are you feeling about your work right now? Tell me more."
3
Coaching Through Challenges
When they bring a problem, resist the urge to solve it immediately. Coach first.
"What have you already tried?"
"What do you think the root cause is?"
"What options do you see from where you're standing?"
"What would you do if I wasn't here to ask?"
"What support do you actually need from me here?"
4
Giving Radical Candor Feedback
Care Personally + Challenge Directly. Specific, timely, always a dialogue.
Be specific: reference a real moment, not a pattern or personality.
Name the behaviour and the impact — not the person.
Always end with: "How did it feel from your side?"
Praise in the moment — don't save it all for the 1-to-1.
Developmental feedback: private and close to the moment.
5
Inviting Feedback on You
Two-way Radical Candor doesn't happen by accident — ask for it explicitly.
"Is there anything I could do differently to make your job easier?"
"What's one thing I do that's genuinely helpful — and one that gets in the way?"
"Am I giving you enough clarity / autonomy / support right now?"
When you receive feedback: thank them, don't defend. Reflect before you respond.
✓
Closing Every 1-to-1 Well
The last two minutes matter more than most managers realise.

End of Session Checklist

1
Summarise key decisions and actions — who owns what, by when
2
"Is there anything we didn't get to that you wanted to raise?"
3
Confirm any commitments you're making on your side
4
Note one thing to follow up on before the next session
5
Protect the next session in the diary before you leave
