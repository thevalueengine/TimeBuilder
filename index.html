Before we get started

This tool is powered by Claude, an AI made by Anthropic. When you use it — including any messages you send and any report you choose to upload — that information is sent to Anthropic's API to generate your responses.

What Anthropic does with your data: Anthropic does not use API data to train its AI models by default. Your data may be stored temporarily for safety monitoring purposes, in line with Anthropic's privacy policy. The Value Engine does not store your data.

Your report is optional. Uploading it helps personalise responses to your situation, but the tool works without it. Only upload information you are comfortable sharing via a third-party AI service.

1-to-1 guide

How to prepare for a meaningful, focused one-to-one conversation.

Before your 1-to-1

Your 1-to-1 is your time. Come prepared — your manager can't read your mind.

📌
What's on your plate right now?

Think about the projects, tasks, or decisions weighing on you most.

🚧
What's blocking you?

What do you need help with, a decision on, or permission to deprioritise?

💡
What's going well?

Don't skip this — sharing wins helps your manager understand your contribution.

🎯
What do you want from this conversation?

Advice? A decision? Just to be heard? Know what you need before you go in.

Useful questions to bring
"What should I be spending more time on?"
"Is there anything about my work I should know that I might not?"
"What would make me more effective in my role right now?"
"I'm finding X difficult — what would you do?"
Before your 1-to-1

Your job in a 1-to-1 is to listen first, help second. The agenda belongs to them.

👂
Start with their agenda

Ask: "What do you most want to cover today?" Then listen. Don't rush to fill silence.

🔍
Notice what they're not saying

Are they quieter than usual? Vague? Frustrated? That's often where the real conversation is.

🧹
Clear blockers

Your #1 job: remove obstacles. What decisions or resources do they need that only you can provide?

📈
Close with clarity

End every 1-to-1 with agreed next steps. Who does what, by when?

Useful questions to ask
"What's taking up more energy than it should right now?"
"What would make your work easier that I might be able to help with?"
"Is there anything about how I'm managing you that I could do differently?"
"What are you most proud of since we last spoke?"
